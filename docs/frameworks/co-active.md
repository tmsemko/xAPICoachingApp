# Co-Active Coaching — Whole-Person, Relationship-Based Coaching

## Classification

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| **Primary job function** | Executive presence, public speaking & communication coaching; career and life-work coaching |
| **Secondary job functions** | Leadership development, high-potential programs, founder coaching |
| **Coaching style** | Whole-person, non-directive, experiential |
| **Origin** | Laura Whitworth, Karen & Henry Kimsey-House, Phillip Sandahl — *Co-Active Coaching* (1998); institutionalized via the Co-Active Training Institute (CTI), the largest ICF-accredited coach training school |
| **Typical session length** | 45–60 minutes; designed for multi-month engagements |

## Executive Summary

Co-Active is less a session script than a complete coaching philosophy — the foundation of the world's largest professional coach training program and a major influence on ICF competency standards. Its premise: the coachee is "naturally creative, resourceful and whole," and coaching addresses the *person*, not the problem. The model rests on four **Cornerstones**, works through three **Principles** (Fulfillment, Balance, Process), and equips the coach with five **Contexts** (Listening, Intuition, Curiosity, Forward & Deepen, Self-Management). Its three-level listening model is the industry-standard rubric for coach listening skill, and its emphasis on presence, voice, and impact makes it the natural home for public speaking and executive-presence coaching, where *how you show up* is the actual subject matter. The trade-offs: it requires serious training (CTI certification is ~6+ months), sessions don't reliably end in a task list, and it's poor fit for quick operational or metric-driven coaching.

## The Framework

### The Four Cornerstones (assumptions the coach must hold)
1. **People are naturally creative, resourceful and whole** — no fixing, no advice-giving by default.
2. **Focus on the whole person** — the topic they bring sits inside a whole life; coach the person, not the problem.
3. **Dance in this moment** — follow what emerges live (energy, tone, resistance) over any pre-set agenda.
4. **Evoke transformation** — the goal is expanded capacity and self-awareness, not just a solved issue.

### The Three Principles (what engagements work on)
- **Fulfillment** — clarifying values and what a deeply alive, meaningful work-life looks like; the values-clarification exercises live here.
- **Balance** — expanding perspectives and choice when the coachee feels stuck or torn; choosing a stance and committing.
- **Process** — being with what is happening *now*, including discomfort; especially relevant for presence and performance-anxiety work.

### The Five Contexts (the coach's toolkit)

**1. Listening — the Three Levels** (Co-Active's most exported concept)

| Level | Attention | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **I — Internal** | On yourself | You relate everything to your own experience; fine for the coachee, a failure mode for the coach |
| **II — Focused** | On the other | Laser attention on the coachee's words, meaning, and emotion |
| **III — Global** | On everything | Attending to energy, tone, body language, what's *not* said — the room itself |

Professional coaching happens at Levels II and III.

**2. Intuition** — offering hunches lightly ("I have a hunch — may I share it?") and letting the coachee take or leave them.

**3. Curiosity** — powerful, open, short questions asked from genuine not-knowing, not diagnosis.

**4. Forward the Action & Deepen the Learning** — the framework's dual output: every session should produce both movement (actions, requests, challenges) and learning (self-awareness the coachee keeps). Tools include inquiries (a big question to live with between sessions), requests, and challenges.

**5. Self-Management** — the coach's ability to set aside their own opinions, judgments, and need to look good.

## Rubric

Co-Active's listening levels function as a native rubric, and because CTI training feeds ICF certification, Co-Active coaches are commonly assessed against ICF-style competency scales. Both are included, plus a public-speaking application rubric.

### Listening Level Rubric (assess the coach, per session)

| Rating | Observed behavior |
|---|---|
| **Level I dominant** | Coach relates coachee's material to own experience, gives advice, tells stories about themselves |
| **Level II intermittent** | Focused listening with lapses into problem-solving or self-reference |
| **Level II sustained** | Consistent focused attention; reflections track the coachee's words and emotions accurately |
| **Level III** | Coach also works with energy, tone, pace, and the unsaid; names shifts in the moment ("something changed when you said that") |

### Co-Active Session Rubric (score each row 1–4)

| Dimension | 1 — Developing | 2 — Emerging | 3 — Proficient | 4 — Exemplary |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Coaching the person vs. the problem** | Session is pure problem-solving | Mostly topic-focused; person appears occasionally | Values, patterns, and identity connected to the topic | Coachee gains self-knowledge transferable far beyond the topic |
| **Listening level** | Level I dominant | Level II intermittent | Level II sustained | Level III; unsaid material surfaced |
| **Curiosity & questions** | Leading/diagnostic questions | Open but long or stacked questions | Short, powerful, open questions | Questions evoke visible discovery; comfortable silence used |
| **Dancing in the moment** | Coach drives a fixed agenda | Follows content shifts only | Responds to energy/emotion shifts live | Redesigns the session in the moment around what emerges |
| **Forward & deepen** | Neither action nor learning | Action without learning (or vice versa) | Both action and articulated learning | Inquiry/challenge extends learning between sessions; accountability designed by coachee |

**Scoring guide:** 17–20 masterful (ICF PCC/MCC-like) · 13–16 solid professional coaching · 9–12 consulting in coaching clothing · 5–8 retrain fundamentals.

### Public-Speaking / Executive-Presence Application Rubric

When Co-Active methods are applied to speaking and presence coaching, sessions typically assess the *speaker* on:

| Dimension | 1 — Developing | 2 — Emerging | 3 — Proficient | 4 — Exemplary |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Presence / process** | Reads slides; visibly in head, not room | Moments of contact with audience | Grounded, breathing, present under pressure | Recovers from disruption gracefully; presence is felt |
| **Authenticity (fulfillment)** | Performs a persona | Genuine in low-stakes moments | Message aligned with own values and voice | Personal conviction visibly moves the audience |
| **Range (balance)** | One register regardless of content | Some vocal/physical variation | Deliberate shifts in pace, tone, stance by section | Full expressive range matched to meaning |
| **Connection (level II/III)** | Talks *at* the audience | Notices audience but doesn't adjust | Reads the room and adapts live | Two-way energy; audience behavior shifts during the talk |

## Best Use Case

**Executive presence, public speaking, and career-transition coaching — any engagement where the person, their voice, and how they show up *is* the subject.** A leader with strong content but flat delivery, a founder preparing for fundraising keynotes, or a high-potential deciding what they actually want from their career gets more from Co-Active's whole-person depth than from any session-script model. Use GROW/FUEL/OSKAR for operational performance topics; Co-Active requires a trained coach and multi-session runway to pay off.
