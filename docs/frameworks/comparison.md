# Coaching Frameworks — Comparison & Selection Guide

Companion to the five framework files in this directory: [GROW](grow.md), [CLEAR](clear.md), [OSKAR](oskar.md), [FUEL](fuel.md), [Co-Active](co-active.md).

## Executive Summaries

### GROW (Goal · Reality · Options · Will)
The world's default coaching model (Whitmore, 1992). Four movements take a coachee from a stated goal, through an honest look at current reality, to self-generated options and a committed action. Simple enough to teach any manager in an afternoon, structured enough to guarantee every conversation ends with a next step. Best all-purpose starting point; weakest where issues are emotional or the coachee lacks the knowledge to generate options.

### CLEAR (Contracting · Listening · Exploring · Action · Review)
Hawkins' transformational model, built for changing how leaders think and feel rather than just what they do. Adds what GROW omits: an explicit contract at the start and a review of the coaching itself at the end, with deep listening and exploration of emotions and underlying beliefs in between. The executive-coaching workhorse; heavier and slower than needed for routine operational topics.

### OSKAR (Outcome · Scaling · Know-how · Affirm & Action · Review)
The leading solution-focused model (McKergow & Jackson, 2002). Skips problem analysis entirely: define the desired outcome, scale current position 0–10, mine the score for what's *already working*, affirm strengths with evidence, and take a small step up the scale. Fast, energizing, measurable across sessions — ideal for teams and confidence dips; deliberately wrong for problems that need root-cause diagnosis.

### FUEL (Frame · Understand · Explore · Lay out a plan)
Zenger & Stinnett's model for the conversations managers must *initiate* — feedback, performance, sales call and deal debriefs. Forces the two steps managers habitually skip: framing the purpose of the conversation upfront and understanding the employee's view before offering their own. Dominant in sales enablement because it maps directly onto call-review and pipeline cadences; less suited to deep developmental work.

### Co-Active (Cornerstones · Fulfillment/Balance/Process · Five Contexts)
The whole-person philosophy behind the world's largest coach-training school (CTI) and much of ICF's competency model. Coaches the person, not the problem, through three-level listening, curiosity, intuition, and "forward the action / deepen the learning." The natural home for executive presence, public speaking, and career coaching — where how someone shows up *is* the topic. Requires real training and multi-session engagements.

## Classification by Job Function

| Job function | Best fit | Also works | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Management / people leadership (1:1s, development)** | GROW | FUEL, OSKAR | Lightest training load; every conversation yields a committed action |
| **Sales coaching (call debriefs, deal reviews, pipeline)** | FUEL | GROW | Frame step legitimizes manager-initiated feedback; plan step ties to sales cadence |
| **Executive & senior leadership coaching** | CLEAR | Co-Active | Contracting + depth work reach the beliefs beneath the presenting issue |
| **Public speaking / executive presence / communication** | Co-Active | CLEAR (rehearsal step) | Presence, voice, and authenticity are the subject; three-level listening and process work address them directly |
| **Team performance / agile & project teams** | OSKAR | GROW | Scaling gives a shared, trackable measure; strengths focus prevents blame spirals |
| **Performance management / difficult feedback** | FUEL | CLEAR | Built for coach-initiated conversations with accountability follow-up |
| **Career development / transitions** | Co-Active | GROW | Values and fulfillment work precede goal-setting |
| **Customer success / support team coaching** | OSKAR | FUEL | Brief, repeatable, morale-positive cadence |

## Compare & Contrast

### At a glance

| | GROW | CLEAR | OSKAR | FUEL | Co-Active |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Orientation** | Goal-driven | Relationship/transformation | Solution-focused | Conversation-framing | Whole-person |
| **Unit of change** | Behavior toward a goal | Beliefs & patterns | What's already working | Skill + accountability | The person & self-awareness |
| **Who sets the agenda** | Coachee | Negotiated (contract) | Coachee | Coach may initiate | Coachee (whole life in scope) |
| **Session length** | 20–60 min | 45–90 min | 15–45 min | 10–45 min | 45–60 min |
| **Engagement shape** | One-off or recurring | Recurring engagement | Short loop, repeated | Cadence-driven (pipeline, reviews) | Multi-month engagement |
| **Training required** | Hours | Days–weeks | Hours–days | Hours–days | Months (certification) |
| **Built-in measurement** | Commitment scale (1–10) | Depth-of-engagement levels | 0–10 outcome scale, tracked | Success plan w/ follow-up date | Listening levels; ICF-style competencies |
| **Handles problem analysis?** | Yes (Reality) | Yes, deeply | Deliberately no | Yes (Understand) | Reframed to the person |
| **Directive-friendly?** | Low | Low | Low | Medium (by design) | Very low |

### Key contrasts

- **Problem vs. solution orientation.** GROW, CLEAR, and FUEL all examine current reality before moving forward; OSKAR explicitly refuses to, spending that time on what already works. Co-Active sidesteps the axis entirely by coaching the person rather than the issue.
- **Who starts the conversation.** GROW, OSKAR, and Co-Active assume the coachee brings the topic. FUEL is the only model engineered for manager-initiated conversations — its Frame step exists precisely because feedback delivered without framing lands as an ambush. CLEAR's contracting handles both directions but takes longer.
- **Depth vs. speed.** OSKAR and FUEL are built for 15–30 minute recurring conversations; GROW sits in the middle; CLEAR and Co-Active trade speed for depth, working at the level of beliefs (CLEAR) and identity/values (Co-Active). Matching depth to the actual need is the main selection error: using CLEAR for a call debrief wastes an hour, and using FUEL on a crisis of confidence treats a person like a pipeline stage.
- **Structure vs. philosophy.** GROW, OSKAR, FUEL, and CLEAR are session scripts you can run tomorrow. Co-Active is a trained discipline — its rubrics (listening levels) assess the *coach*, which is also why its concepts underpin professional coach certification while the others dominate manager-as-coach programs.
- **What "success" means.** GROW: a committed action. FUEL: a success plan with a follow-up date. OSKAR: measurable movement up a scale. CLEAR: a shift in thinking, rehearsed. Co-Active: action *plus* durable learning about oneself.

## Best Use Case per Framework

| Framework | Choose it when... | Avoid it when... |
|---|---|---|
| **GROW** | A manager runs regular 1:1 development conversations and needs a reliable, trainable default | The issue is emotional/relational, or the coachee lacks knowledge to generate options |
| **CLEAR** | An executive's presenting problem masks a deeper pattern; sensitive or high-stakes conversations | You need speed; routine operational topics |
| **OSKAR** | Teams are fatigued, confidence is low, and you want fast, measurable, blame-free progress | The problem needs genuine root-cause diagnosis (technical, compliance) |
| **FUEL** | Sales debriefs, deal reviews, or any feedback conversation the manager must initiate | The topic is developmental depth, identity, or long-term growth |
| **Co-Active** | Public speaking, executive presence, or career work — where the person is the subject | You need quick, task-oriented outcomes or lack a trained coach |

## A practical pairing note

These frameworks are complementary, not competing. A common organizational stack: **GROW** as the manager-wide default, **FUEL** for sales and performance conversations, **OSKAR** for team cadences, and **CLEAR** or **Co-Active** reserved for professional/executive coaching engagements. Coaches also nest them — e.g., running a GROW arc inside a CLEAR contract, or using OSKAR's scaling question during GROW's Reality phase.
