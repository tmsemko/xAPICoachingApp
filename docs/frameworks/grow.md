# GROW — Goal, Reality, Options, Will

## Classification

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| **Primary job function** | Management / people leadership |
| **Secondary job functions** | Sales coaching, career development, public speaking prep, athletic & skills coaching |
| **Coaching style** | Non-directive, goal-oriented |
| **Origin** | Sir John Whitmore, Graham Alexander, Alan Fine (1980s); popularized in *Coaching for Performance* (1992) |
| **Typical session length** | 20–60 minutes; works for one-off or recurring sessions |

## Executive Summary

GROW is the most widely used coaching framework in the world and the default model taught in manager-as-coach programs. It structures a conversation into four movements: define the **Goal**, examine current **Reality**, generate **Options**, and commit to a **Will** (way forward). Its power is its simplicity — a first-line manager can learn it in an afternoon, yet it scales to executive coaching. GROW assumes the coachee has the answers and the coach's job is to unlock them through questions, not advice. Its weakness is that it can feel mechanical if applied as a checklist, and it under-serves situations requiring deep emotional exploration or where the coachee lacks the underlying knowledge to generate options.

## The Framework

### G — Goal
Establish what the coachee wants to achieve — for the session, the short term, and the long term. Goals should be inspirational and stated positively, then made specific.

Key questions:
- What do you want to achieve from this conversation?
- What does success look like? How will you know you've achieved it?
- Is this goal within your control? If not, what part of it is?
- When do you want to achieve it by?

### R — Reality
Explore the current situation objectively. This is often the longest phase — many issues resolve once reality is clearly seen.

Key questions:
- What is happening now? What have you tried so far?
- What results did that produce?
- What obstacles are in your way? Which are internal vs. external?
- On a scale of 1–10, how far along are you toward the goal?

### O — Options
Generate the widest possible range of alternatives before evaluating any of them.

Key questions:
- What could you do? What else? (ask at least three times)
- If constraints (time, budget, authority) disappeared, what would you do?
- What has worked for you in a similar situation?
- Would you like a suggestion from me? (advice only by permission)

### W — Will (or Way Forward / Wrap-up)
Convert insight into commitment: specific actions, timing, and accountability.

Key questions:
- What will you do? By when?
- On a scale of 1–10, how committed are you to this action? What would make it a 10?
- What support do you need, and from whom?
- How will we follow up?

## Rubric

Whitmore's original text does not ship a formal scoring rubric; the rubric below is the widely used adaptation for evaluating GROW conversations in manager-as-coach and coach-certification programs.

### GROW Conversation Quality Rubric (score each row 1–4)

| Dimension | 1 — Developing | 2 — Emerging | 3 — Proficient | 4 — Exemplary |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Goal clarity** | No explicit goal; conversation drifts | Goal named but vague or coach-imposed | Coachee states a specific, measurable session goal | Session goal linked to longer-term performance/development goal owned by coachee |
| **Reality exploration** | Coach assumes or tells the situation | Superficial questions; coach jumps to solutions | Open questions surface facts, feelings, and obstacles | Coachee reaches a self-generated insight about the real barrier |
| **Option generation** | Coach prescribes the answer | One or two options, mostly from coach | Coachee generates 3+ options before evaluating | Coachee expands beyond initial assumptions; advice offered only by permission |
| **Will / commitment** | No action agreed | Vague intention ("I'll try to...") | Specific action, owner, and deadline agreed | Action + commitment scaled (8+/10), support identified, follow-up scheduled |
| **Talk ratio & questioning** | Coach talks >60% | Coach talks ~50%; frequent closed/leading questions | Coachee talks ~70%; mostly open questions | Coachee talks 80%+; short, powerful questions; comfortable silence |

**Scoring guide:** 17–20 exemplary coaching · 13–16 proficient · 9–12 emerging (target one dimension for practice) · 5–8 needs foundational training.

### Coachee commitment scale (built into the Will phase)
Ask: *"On a scale of 1–10, how likely are you to do this?"* — any answer below 8 signals the action should be resized or the obstacle re-explored before closing the session.

## Best Use Case

**A manager running regular 1:1 performance and development conversations with direct reports.** GROW is the best first framework for any organization introducing a coaching culture: lowest training overhead, works across functions (sales call debriefs, presentation prep, career conversations), and produces a concrete committed action every session. Choose something else when the topic is deeply relational/emotional (see CLEAR) or when the coachee is stuck in problem-talk (see OSKAR).
